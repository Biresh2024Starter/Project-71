School Libraries

Were established in 1954.

South Point School Library (Mandeville Gardens): The Mandeville Gardens campus houses a well-stocked library with a collection of nearly 18,000 volumes. The library includes: Reference Books: Encyclopedias, encyclopedic dictionaries, and informative picture books. Reading Material: Nursery books, mathematics books, song books, and CDs. Comics and Cartoons: Children can enjoy comics and cartoon books within the library itself. The library provides a conducive environment for learning and exploration. South Point High School Library (Ballygunge): The Ballygunge campus features a comprehensive library with approximately 35,000 titles. The library includes: Periodicals and Newspapers: Regularly updated reading material. Reference Titles: Subject-specific and general reference books. Competitive Exam Help-Books: Useful for students preparing for competitive examinations. Students can either borrow books or browse through them within the library. Both libraries play a crucial role in fostering knowledge, curiosity, and a love for reading among students.

School Achievements

Launched Its Own Satellite Named PriyamvadaSat from ISRO.
South Point High School, located in Kolkata, has taken a historic step into space exploration by launching its own student nano-satellite named PriyamvadaSat. This initiative marks the school's 70th anniversary and coincides with India's 75th year of independence. The satellite, named in memory of late Smt. Priyamvada Birla, former President of South Point Education Society, aims to provide students with hands-on experience in space science and coding. Additionally, the school will establish a Space Lab on its premises, allowing students to monitor PriyamvadaSat and engage in standard lab practices. This exciting venture brings the future closer to South Point's students.
Won The Telegraph School of the Year Award in 1971
South Point High School achieved the prestigious TT School of the Year award in 1971. This remarkable achievement was a testament to their dedication and excellence in table tennis.
South Point School wins the Guinnuess World Record as the world's largest school (in terms of students) (1984-1992)
South Point School (SPS), secured a place in the Guinness Book of Records as the world's largest school. This incredible achievement reflects SPS's commitment to education, growth, and fostering a vibrant learning environment for thousands of students!

What's New?

  • Restructuring of Trustee Board

    The South Point Education Society restructured its trustee board and governing council by removing Krishna Damani. Krishna Damani was arrested on charges of misappropriation of funds. Three new members have been inducted into the trust: physicist and author Partha Ghose, former bureaucrat and former Coal India chairman Sutirtha Bhattacharya, and Pradip Tondon from the MP Birla Group. The police are investigating alleged embezzlement of funds at South Point School. Damani's kin received funds from the school trust and distributed them to different family members. Forged documents were found, and custodial interrogation is ongoing to recover evidence

  • Space Exploration and Training

    South Point High School in Kolkata will be training 44 students to use satellite data. They aim to understand the changing nature of the Sundarbans and East Kolkata Wetlands. The school plans to build a ground station after the satellite launch
